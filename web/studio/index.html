Studio

Businesses in the directory bring their marketing to Box Fraise. Every artifact — posters, photography, cards, menus, signage, websites — goes through the studio, because doing marketing outside the network you belong to would be strange.

Fees are standardized across the network. The designer is employed by Box Fraise, not contracted or freelanced — one person who knows the businesses in the network and does their work well.

Case study · design

Harubaku Coffee Roasters

A specialty coffee roaster in Edson, Alberta, specialising in small-batch, woodfire-roasted coffee using Japanese charcoal techniques. The craft is real; the marketing hasn't caught up to it. The storefront currently carries a coming-soon poster that reads as AI-generated composite art — the kind of default a small business reaches for when there's no one whose job it is to make something better.

Before · storefront window
After · redesigned

This is a design study reimagining both the storefront poster and the website in Box Fraise's editorial aesthetic — Newsreader italic display, mono accents, generous whitespace, product photography given the room to speak. Four artifacts: home, coffee, story, and a redesigned coming-soon poster to sit in the window in place of the one above.
