Legal Justice Fund

Donation-based · not a registered charity · contributions are not tax-deductible

Box Fraise runs a donation-based fund with three purposes.

It pays members of the legal community to speak at events held by Box Fraise. Legal thought is often heard only by other lawyers, in closed rooms and paywalled proceedings. The fund brings it into public rooms where anyone can listen and ask questions back.

It pays for the homeless community to receive haircuts every Sunday at the same chairs the legal community sits in during the week. The salon does not close on Sunday for this — it opens differently.

It contributes to running the project. Coordinating speakers, opening the salon on Sundays, and holding the wider Box Fraise project together — the fund covers those operational costs alongside the two named programs. Donors contribute to the whole apparatus that makes those programs possible, not only the visible half.

How to give

Donations are accepted in person at Box Fraise events. There is no online channel today — setting one up properly, with registration, receipts, banking, and compliance in place, takes money the project doesn't yet have. Shipping a half-built version of that infrastructure would be worse than shipping none. Come to an event, meet the people running it, and contribute if you're moved to.

The fund is named for the tension the salon is built around: a legal community expected to serve everyone, and a population — the homeless — that legal services rarely reach. The fund pays for both to sit in the same chair.
